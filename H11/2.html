Curriculum

The school provides a wide range of subject choices, allowing all students to have the opportunity to achieve their goals of entering universities, technical colleges, or employment through flexible and diverse means. Extracurricular activities are also very diverse, providing students with growth opportunities in sports, culture, or other talents such as debate. Students receive comprehensive quality education, both inside and outside the classroom, which helps cultivate their lifelong learning habits. The atmosphere of the school is based on good relationships with classmates, teachers, and parents, as well as good relationships with the outside society. The basic school culture is mutual respect, support, and cooperation. Every grade can be exposed to the application of technology, and students use laptops or desktop computers extensively in their daily learning.

Preparatory course placement

The High School Preparation Program (HSP) at Canter College aims to train students in the language skills required to integrate into Australian courses in junior high (grades 7-9) or high school (grades 10-12). Students in the initial stage of receiving international education can expect high levels of personal support and care. Our HSP course provides students with a comprehensive language foundation, with organized core content covering English, mathematics, science, business, and physical education for 6 hours per day. Students can transition to a mixed curriculum of HSP subjects and mainstream classes, and those subjects that pass the English proficiency test can be taught together with mainstream classes.
